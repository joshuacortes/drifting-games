Modified Toyota MR2 Builds

The Toyota MR2, with its mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive layout, has been a favorite among enthusiasts for modification. The second-generation MR2 (1990-1999) and the third-generation MR2 Spyder (1999-2007) are particularly popular choices. These MR2 models are often modified with engine swaps, turbocharging, and suspension upgrades to enhance their performance and handling capabilities. The MR2's compact and sporty design, combined with its spirited driving dynamics, continues to attract those seeking to create exhilarating, lightweight sports cars.



Toyota MR2 Build Kit

The Toyota MR2 is a legendary sports car that has captured the hearts of automotive enthusiasts since its inception. Known for its mid-engine layout, superb handling, and thrilling driving experience, the MR2 has become a cult classic in the automotive world. For those looking to personalize and upgrade their MR2, there's a wide range of build kits and aftermarket products available to enhance the performance, appearance, and overall driving experience of this iconic car.


3SGTE Engine Swap Kits

One of the most popular upgrades for Toyota MR2 enthusiasts is the 3SGTE engine swap. The 3SGTE is a turbocharged four-cylinder engine that provides a significant boost in power over the stock engine. Several brands offer comprehensive engine swap kits that include everything you need to make the conversion, such as wiring harnesses, ECU, and engine mounts. Prime examples include ATS Racing, Monkeywrench Racing, and KO Racing, each offering their own unique kits and components to suit your specific needs.


Coilover Suspension Kits

Improving the handling and ride quality of your MR2 is essential, and a coilover suspension kit can make a world of difference. Brands like KW Suspensions, Tein, and HKS offer adjustable coilover systems designed to fine-tune your MR2's suspension. These kits allow you to lower your car's ride height, improve cornering capabilities, and achieve the perfect balance between comfort and performance.


Body Kits and Aerodynamics

If you want to give your MR2 a more aggressive and stylish appearance, there are numerous body kit options available. Brands like Veilside, Bomex, and TRD offer body kits that include front and rear bumpers, side skirts, spoilers, and other aerodynamic enhancements. These kits not only enhance the aesthetics of your MR2 but also improve its stability at high speeds.


Exhaust Systems

Upgrading your MR2's exhaust system can improve performance, enhance the engine's note, and reduce weight. Brands like HKS, Greddy, and Borla produce high-quality exhaust systems tailored to the MR2. Whether you prefer a deep and throaty exhaust tone or a more refined sound, you can find an exhaust system to match your preferences and boost your car's performance.


Wheels and Tires

A new set of wheels and tires can completely transform the look and handling of your MR2. Brands like Enkei, Volk Racing, and Rota Wheels offer a wide range of designs and sizes to suit your style and performance needs. Pair them with high-performance tires from manufacturers like Michelin, Bridgestone, or Toyo Tires to maximize grip and handling capabilities.


Interior Upgrades

Enhancing the interior of your MR2 can greatly improve your driving experience. Brands like Sparco, Recaro, and Bride offer racing seats that provide improved support and comfort during spirited driving. Additionally, you can upgrade the steering wheel, shift knob, and add custom gauge clusters to personalize your MR2's cockpit.


Braking Upgrades

Enhancing your MR2's braking system is essential for improved performance and safety. Brands like Brembo, Wilwood, and StopTech offer brake kits with larger rotors, calipers, and performance brake pads. These upgrades provide better stopping power and reduce brake fade during aggressive driving.

Toyota MR2 Spyder Build

Design and Body

The Toyota MR2 Spyder, known as the MR-S in some markets, is a testament to the principle that good things come in small packages. The design is characterized by its sporty, low-slung, and aerodynamic body. It's a two-seater roadster with a compact footprint, giving it a nimble and agile appearance. The MR2 Spyder's mid-engine layout not only adds to its visual appeal but also contributes to its exceptional balance and handling.

One of the most striking features of the MR2 Spyder is its convertible soft top. With a few easy steps, the roof can be retracted, transforming the car from a closed coupe into an open-top roadster. This versatility allows drivers to savor the joys of driving with the wind in their hair while enjoying the sun on a clear day.


Performance

The 2001 Toyota MR2 Spyder is equipped with a 1.8-liter inline-four engine, which delivers 138 horsepower and 125 lb-ft of torque. While these numbers might not sound groundbreaking, the lightweight design of the MR2 Spyder, coupled with its mid-engine layout and rear-wheel drive, ensures that it is an absolute joy to drive. The car's excellent power-to-weight ratio means it can sprint from 0 to 60 mph in around 7 seconds, making it a responsive and lively roadster.

What truly sets the MR2 Spyder apart, however, is its exceptional handling. The mid-engine configuration ensures a near-perfect 50/50 weight distribution, enhancing stability and cornering prowess. Its responsive steering and precise feedback make it a favorite among driving enthusiasts. The MR2 Spyder feels connected to the road, allowing the driver to experience the thrill of every turn and curve.


Interior and Comfort

The interior of the MR2 Spyder is simple and functional, emphasizing the driver's connection to the road. The cabin is compact, with two well-bolstered sport seats that hold occupants securely in place during spirited driving. While the interior materials may not be luxurious, they are durable and well-suited to the car's performance-oriented nature.

One unique feature of the MR2 Spyder is the centrally positioned tachometer in the instrument cluster, emphasizing its sports car pedigree. This layout ensures that the driver remains focused on the road and the task at hand.


Legacy and Enthusiast Following

The Toyota MR2 Spyder may have ceased production in 2007, but its legacy lives on. Enthusiast communities continue to thrive, celebrating this roadster for its affordability, reliability, and driving purity. Its simplicity and ease of maintenance have made it a popular choice for those looking to enter the world of sports car ownership without breaking the bank.

Many MR2 Spyders have been modified and tuned to enhance their performance, making them competitive on the track or the autocross course. It's a testament to the car's versatility and potential for customization.

Is a Toyota MR2 a good car?

First Generation (AW11): The AW11 was a compact, lightweight sports car with a mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive layout. It was celebrated for its sharp handling and affordability, making it accessible to a wide range of drivers.

Second Generation (SW20): The SW20, also known as the MR2 Turbo, introduced a more powerful turbocharged engine and a more refined suspension. It continued to offer the same mid-engine layout and sporty driving experience.

Third Generation (W30): The W30, often referred to as the MR2 Spyder or MR-S, brought a fresh design with a convertible soft top. It featured a 1.8-liter engine and a focus on nimble handling and driver engagement.


What Makes the MR2 a Good Car?

Exceptional Handling: The MR2's mid-engine layout, coupled with rear-wheel drive, contributes to its impeccable balance and handling. The car is known for its ability to take corners with precision, making it a favorite for those who appreciate spirited driving.

Affordability: Throughout its various generations, the MR2 has maintained a reputation for being relatively affordable in the sports car segment. This makes it an attractive option for those looking to experience the thrill of a sports car without breaking the bank.

Reliability: Toyota's reputation for reliability extends to the MR2. These cars are known to be durable and dependable, provided they are well-maintained.

Unique Design: The MR2's distinctive design, with its mid-engine silhouette and sleek lines, sets it apart from other sports cars. It exudes a sense of timeless style that continues to turn heads.

Enthusiast Community: The MR2 has a dedicated and passionate enthusiast community. This means that support, knowledge, and a wide range of aftermarket parts are readily available for those looking to modify or restore their MR2.


Is a Toyota MR2 a good car? Absolutely. It's a car that has carved out its own niche in the world of sports cars. Its combination of exceptional handling, affordability, reliability, unique design, and an enthusiastic community of supporters makes it a car that stands the test of time. If you're in search of a sports car that provides both driving thrills and practicality, the Toyota MR2 is indeed a compelling choice.

Is the Toyota MR2 a rare car?

Production Numbers:

The rarity of a car often stems from its production numbers. In the case of the Toyota MR2, while it was not produced in massive quantities like some mainstream models, it wasn't exactly a limited-edition supercar either. During its production run, each generation of the MR2 found its share of admirers, with production numbers varying across the years. The first-generation (AW11) saw more than 160,000 units produced, the second generation (SW20) had over 150,000 units, and the third generation (W30) had approximately 139,000 units manufactured. These numbers, while not enormous, don't categorize the MR2 as extremely rare when compared to some other sports cars.


Aging and Survivorship:

The rarity of a car often increases as it ages and experiences natural attrition. The Toyota MR2, especially the earlier generations, is now several decades old. This has led to many cars succumbing to the wear and tear of time, accidents, or simply being retired to scrapyards. As a result, finding well-preserved, low-mileage MR2s, especially in the case of the first and second generations, has become increasingly challenging. The survival rate of these cars plays a significant role in defining their rarity.


Enthusiast Market:

The demand for a particular model in the used car market also influences its perceived rarity. The Toyota MR2 has a strong and dedicated enthusiast community. Enthusiasts often seek out well-maintained examples, causing a higher demand for the remaining MR2s. The popularity of the MR2 among collectors and modifiers has contributed to the perception of rarity.


Third-Generation Exception:

It's important to note that the third-generation MR2, the MR2 Spyder or MR-S, tends to be perceived as rarer than its predecessors due to a shorter production run. This generation, with its convertible soft top, offered a unique driving experience but had limited time in the market. As a result, finding well-preserved MR-S models is relatively less common.


Geographic Variations:

The rarity of the Toyota MR2 can also vary significantly by geographic location. In some regions, particularly those with harsh climates, these cars may be more challenging to find in good condition, further enhancing their scarcity in those areas.

Is a Toyota MR2 a good drift car?

Mid-Engine Configuration:

One of the key attributes that make the MR2 an intriguing choice for drifting is its mid-engine layout. This unique setup places the engine between the axles, resulting in nearly perfect weight distribution. This balanced weight distribution enhances the MR2's ability to transition into slides and drifts, as the rear wheels can break traction more predictably, thanks to the weight over them. This design contributes to exceptional handling characteristics, which is vital for controlled drifting.


Responsive Handling:

The MR2's responsive and direct steering, along with its lightweight construction, provides an excellent platform for drifters. The car's nimble nature allows for precise control during drift maneuvers. Enthusiasts often praise the MR2 for its "point and shoot" handling, making it a great tool for executing drifts with finesse.


Engine Options:

The MR2 has seen different engine options across its generations. Enthusiasts particularly favor models equipped with the turbocharged 3S-GTE engine, such as the SW20 generation. The torquey and responsive power delivery of these engines is conducive to drifting. In addition, the tunability and aftermarket support for these powerplants make it relatively easy to increase power for more aggressive drifts.


Aftermarket Support:

The Toyota MR2 benefits from a robust aftermarket support network. You'll find a plethora of performance parts, suspension upgrades, and tuning options to fine-tune your MR2 for drifting. From coilovers to upgraded sway bars, these components can help enhance your car's drifting capabilities.


Challenges and Considerations:

While the Toyota MR2 has numerous advantages as a drift car, it's important to be aware of potential challenges:

Oversteer: The MR2's propensity for oversteer, while desirable for drifting, can also be tricky to manage, especially for novice drifters. It requires a good understanding of throttle control and countersteering.

Snap Oversteer: Some MR2s are known for snap oversteer, which means the rear end can break loose abruptly. Proper modifications and driver skill are essential to control this behavior.

Maintenance and Upkeep: Like any older sports car, the MR2 may require regular maintenance and care to ensure it's in optimal drifting condition.

The Toyota MR2 can be an excellent drift car for those with the right skills and experience. Its mid-engine layout, responsive handling, and various engine options offer a solid foundation for drifting. However, it's essential to approach drifting with the necessary caution and knowledge, as the MR2's oversteer characteristics demand skillful driving to harness its full potential on the drift circuit. With the right setup and a skilled driver behind the wheel, the Toyota MR2 can indeed be a good drift car.

What year was the last Toyota MR2 made?

The last Toyota MR2, also known as the MR2 Spyder or MR-S, was produced in the year 2007. The third generation of the MR2, represented by the MR2 Spyder (W30), marked the final chapter of this beloved sports car's production. Toyota discontinued the MR2 after the 2007 model year, and no new MR2 models have been manufactured since then.

Why was the Toyota MR2 discontinued?

The discontinuation of the Toyota MR2 can be attributed to a combination of factors, primarily market demand and changing automotive industry trends. Here are some key reasons why Toyota chose to discontinue the MR2:

Shifting Market Preferences: During the 2000s, there was a noticeable shift in consumer preferences away from small, lightweight sports cars like the MR2. Many buyers were gravitating towards larger vehicles, such as SUVs and crossovers, as well as hybrid and fuel-efficient models. This shift in demand for more practical and family-oriented vehicles made it less economically viable for Toyota to continue producing the MR2.

Global Economic Conditions: Economic factors also played a role in the decision. The global financial crisis in the late 2000s had a significant impact on the automotive industry, causing many automakers to reevaluate their product lineups and focus on more profitable segments.

Evolving Safety and Emission Regulations: The automotive industry was undergoing changes in safety and emissions regulations, particularly in developed markets. Meeting these new standards required significant investments in research and development. Smaller, niche sports cars like the MR2 had limited economies of scale, making it challenging to justify the necessary investments.

Competitive Landscape: The MR2 faced competition from other sports car models, and sales figures were not as strong as they once were. This, coupled with the cost of keeping the model updated and competitive, played a role in its discontinuation.

Successor Models: Toyota's product lineup was evolving, with new models like the Toyota 86 (also known as the Scion FR-S and Subaru BRZ) catering to sports car enthusiasts. The Toyota 86, which was introduced after the MR2 was discontinued, filled the gap for a compact, rear-wheel-drive sports car in Toyota's lineup.

What is so special about the Toyota MR2?

The Toyota MR2, often affectionately referred to as the "Mister Two," holds a special place in the hearts of automotive enthusiasts for several distinct reasons. Its unique characteristics and design elements make it stand out in the world of sports cars. Here are some of the aspects that make the Toyota MR2 special:

Mid-Engine Layout: The most distinguishing feature of the MR2 is its mid-engine layout. Unlike most cars, which have their engines in the front, the MR2's engine is positioned behind the seats but ahead of the rear axle. This configuration results in near-perfect weight distribution, enhancing the car's balance and handling characteristics. The mid-engine layout is typically associated with high-performance and exotic sports cars, and the MR2 offers a more affordable entry into this world.

Agile Handling: The mid-engine layout, combined with rear-wheel drive, provides the MR2 with exceptional agility. Its nimble and responsive handling allows drivers to take corners with precision, making it a joy to drive on twisty roads or the racetrack. Enthusiasts often praise the MR2 for its sharp steering and impressive maneuverability.

Affordability: Unlike many high-end sports cars, the MR2 has remained relatively affordable throughout its production history. This accessibility makes it an attractive option for car enthusiasts looking for a thrilling driving experience without breaking the bank.

Diverse Generations: The MR2 has undergone three distinct generations, each with its unique characteristics. This diversity allows enthusiasts to choose from various models, whether they prefer the classic lines of the first generation, the turbocharged power of the second generation, or the open-top driving experience of the third generation MR-S (MR2 Spyder).

Strong Enthusiast Community: The MR2 has a passionate and dedicated enthusiast community. This community provides support, knowledge, and a sense of camaraderie among MR2 owners. It's a valuable resource for maintenance and modification advice, as well as finding like-minded enthusiasts to share the MR2 experience.

Iconic Design: The MR2's design, particularly in the first and second generations, is iconic. With its wedge-shaped profile, pop-up headlights (in the first generation), and sleek lines, the MR2 has a timeless and unique appearance that turns heads.

Rarity and Collectibility: While the MR2 wasn't produced in vast quantities, it has gained status as a collectible car. Certain models, especially turbocharged variants and limited editions, are considered more valuable and sought after by collectors.

Customization and Modification Potential: The MR2 offers a wide range of customization and modification possibilities. Enthusiasts can enhance the car's performance, appearance, and handling to suit their individual preferences.

Is the Toyota MR2 a collectible?

Yes, the Toyota MR2 is considered a collectible car by many enthusiasts and collectors. Several factors contribute to its status as a collectible:

Unique Mid-Engine Layout: The MR2's mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive layout sets it apart from most other cars. This distinctive configuration is often associated with high-performance and exotic sports cars, making the MR2 appealing to collectors who appreciate its unique design.

Classic Design: The first and second-generation MR2 models, especially, have classic and iconic designs. Their wedge-shaped profiles, pop-up headlights (in the first generation), and sleek lines have a timeless quality that contributes to their collectibility.

Diverse Generations: The MR2 has gone through three generations, each with its unique characteristics. Collectors have the opportunity to choose from a variety of models, including the classic first-generation AW11, the turbocharged second-generation SW20, and the open-top MR-S (MR2 Spyder) from the third generation.

Limited Editions: Toyota occasionally produced limited-edition MR2 models, which are highly sought after by collectors. These limited editions often feature unique colors, trim packages, and performance enhancements.

Passionate Enthusiast Community: The MR2 has a dedicated and passionate enthusiast community. This community contributes to the car's collectibility by preserving and celebrating the model through events, forums, and social gatherings.

Rarity: While not as rare as some exotic sports cars, the MR2 was not produced in enormous quantities. The limited production numbers, especially for specific models or trim levels, enhance the car's collectible status.

Modification Potential: The MR2 offers substantial modification potential, attracting collectors interested in personalized and unique builds. Many enthusiasts have upgraded their MR2s to enhance performance and appearance, adding to their collectibility.

Historical Significance: As one of Toyota's iconic sports cars, the MR2 has historical significance in the brand's lineup. Its enduring popularity and impact on the sports car market contribute to its collectibility.
